## Design Context

### Users

Self-directed investors and operators reconciling broker confirmations into a clean transaction ledger. They use the app to turn screenshots or PDFs into structured trade rows quickly, with enough confidence to review holdings without extra bookkeeping.

### Brand Personality

Calm, utilitarian, trustworthy. The interface should feel precise and steady rather than flashy or promotional.

### Aesthetic Direction

Bloomberg-lite, but lighter and more approachable. Light-first, information-dense where it matters, with strong contrast and restrained visual treatment. Avoid glossy marketing-site patterns, decorative cards inside cards, or playful consumer-finance styling.

### Brand Colors

Dominant deep teal for trust, focus, and primary actions. Warm bronze as the supporting accent for review states, section separation, and portfolio context. Keep destructive red semantic-only.

### Design Principles

- Put ingestion first; make the upload-to-rows path the clearest action on the page.
- Reduce cognitive load by showing only the decisions and status needed right now.
- Favor compact, well-aligned data presentation over decorative framing.
- Use restrained color and typography to support hierarchy, not to create novelty.
- Respect accessibility with strong contrast, clear labels, keyboard support, and reduced-motion-friendly behavior.
